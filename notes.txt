Lions are the second-largest big cat species in the world, after tigers.
Male lions are easily recognized by their distinctive manes, which signal strength to other lions.
Lions are the only truly social cats, living in groups called prides.
Female lions do most of the hunting for their pride.
A lion's roar can be heard up to 5 miles away.
Lions can sleep up to 20 hours a day.
Lion cubs are born with spots that fade as they grow older.
African lions are listed as vulnerable on the IUCN Red List of Threatened Species.
The scientific name for lions is Panthera leo.
In the wild, lions live for about 10-14 years, but can live up to 20 years in captivity.
